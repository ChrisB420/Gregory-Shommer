The Haitian Flag: Biblical Connections

Theme: Olive Trees, Candlestick, and Prophetic Witnesses

Key Verse: "These are the two olive trees and the two candlesticks standing before the Lord of the earth." — Revelation 11:4
The Haitian Flag in the Bible

Zechariah 4
The line-by-line wording of Zechariah 4 connects to Revelation 11:3-5, unveiling the Haitian flag’s four-square inset as a tapestry of light, depicting the Kingdom of God and His fiery judgment throne. The golden palm tree represents the Menorah’s center (Shamash), symbolizing God, with two olive trees (wagon wheels) as the anointed ones.

Zechariah 4:2
"...behold a candlestick all of gold, with a bowl on the top of it, and seven lamps on it..."
The seven lamps are the six golden triangles and the palm tree, with seven mouths (rectangles) symbolizing the word of God. The two olive trees (wheels) represent eternity (Hebrew number 8).
Revelation 11:4
"These are the two olive trees and the two candlesticks standing before the Lord of the earth."

🔹 Message:

  • Zechariah 4 and Revelation 11 connect the flag’s design to the two witnesses (Jesus and Holy Spirit).
  • The golden palm tree splits a demon head, symbolizing victory over darkness.
  • A spiritual door is opened through praise, removing the curse.

Israel’s Rebirth (1948): A prophetic anchor marking the restoration of God’s chosen nation (Isaiah 66:8).

Haitian Flag & Prophecy: A covenant sign, with colors and symbols foretelling redemption (Zechariah 4:6).
